Wrong Tomorrow [http://wrongtomorrow.com/] by Maciej CegĹ‚owski is a very simple site for listing pundit predictions and tracking them [FAQ [http://wrongtomorrow.com/faq]]. It doesn't come with prices and active betting... but a simple registry of this kind can scale much faster than a market, and right now we're in a situation where no one is bothering to track pundit predictions [http://punditwatch.hubdub.com/] or report on pundit track records. Predictions are produced as simple entertainment [http://lesswrong.com/lw/hi/futuristic_predictions_as_consumable_goods/] or as simple political theater, without the slightest fear of accountability.

This site is missing some features, but it looks to me like a starting attempt at what's needed - a Wikipedia-like, user-contributed, low-barrier-to-entry database of all pundit predictions [http://www.overcomingbias.com/2006/12/the_80_forecast.html], past and present.
