"Can't Say No" Spending

Eliezer Yudkowsky, 18 October 2007 02:08AM

The [http://www.overcomingbias.com/2007/05/rand_health_ins.html] remarkable [http://www.overcomingbias.com/2007/05/rand_health_ins_1.html] observation [http://www.overcomingbias.com/2007/04/overconfidence_.html] that [http://www.overcomingbias.com/2007/05/medicine_as_sca.html] medical [http://www.overcomingbias.com/2007/05/rand_experiment.html] spending [http://www.overcomingbias.com/2007/06/disagreement_ca.html] has [http://www.overcomingbias.com/2007/09/cut-medicine-in.html] zero [http://www.overcomingbias.com/2007/09/beware-monkey-t.html] net [http://www.overcomingbias.com/2007/10/doctors-kill.html] marginal [http://www.overcomingbias.com/2007/10/buy-health-not-.html] effect [http://www.overcomingbias.com/2007/10/health-hope-spr.html] is shocking, but not completely unprecedented.

According to Spiegel in "Too Much of a Good Thing: Choking on Aid Money in Africa [http://www.spiegel.de/international/spiegel/0,1518,363604,00.html]", the Washington Center for Global Development calculated [http://www.spiegel.de/international/spiegel/0,1518,363604-3,00.html] that it would require $3,521 of marginal development aid invested, per person, in order to increase per capita yearly income by $3.65 (one penny per day).

The Kenyan economist James Shikwati is even more pessimistic in "For God's Sake, Please Stop the Aid! [http://www.spiegel.de/international/spiegel/0,1518,363663,00.html]": The net effect of Western aid to Africa is actively destructive (even when it isn't stolen to prop up corrupt regimes), a chaotic flux of money and goods that destroys local industry.
